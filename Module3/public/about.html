The Planets of Our Solar System

Eight worlds. Eight personalities. Scroll to explore quick stats and a cool fact for each planet.

Quick tip: Use your browser’s find feature (Ctrl/Cmd + F) to jump to a planet.

Mercury

  • Type: Rocky (terrestrial)
  • Day length: ~59 Earth days
  • Year length: 88 Earth days
  • Atmosphere: Extremely thin (exosphere)

Cool fact: Mercury has huge temperature swings because it can’t hold onto heat.

Venus

  • Type: Rocky
  • Day length: ~243 Earth days
  • Year length: 225 Earth days
  • Atmosphere: Thick CO₂ with sulfuric acid clouds

Cool fact: Venus spins backwards compared to most planets.

Earth

  • Type: Rocky
  • Day length: 24 hours
  • Year length: 365 days
  • Atmosphere: Nitrogen + oxygen

Cool fact: Earth is the only known world with liquid water on the surface and life (so far!).

Mars

  • Type: Rocky
  • Day length: 24.6 hours
  • Year length: 687 days
  • Atmosphere: Thin CO₂

Cool fact: Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system: Olympus Mons.

Jupiter

  • Type: Gas giant
  • Day length: ~10 hours
  • Year length: ~11.9 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen + helium

Cool fact: Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a massive storm bigger than Earth.

Saturn

  • Type: Gas giant
  • Day length: ~10.7 hours
  • Year length: ~29.5 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen + helium

Cool fact: Saturn’s rings are mostly ice and rock — and they’re super thin compared to their width.

Uranus

  • Type: Ice giant
  • Day length: ~17 hours
  • Year length: ~84 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane

Cool fact: Uranus is tilted on its side, so its seasons are extreme.

Neptune

  • Type: Ice giant
  • Day length: ~16 hours
  • Year length: ~165 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane

Cool fact: Neptune has some of the fastest winds in the solar system.

Bonus: Quick order from the Sun

Mercury → Venus → Earth → Mars → Jupiter → Saturn → Uranus → Neptune
